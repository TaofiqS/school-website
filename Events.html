Black History Month Dinner

On February the 15th Join Stuyvesant BSL and Aspira's annual Black History Month dinner and celebration on Thursday, February 15 at 4pm in the cafeteria. There will be live music, cultural dishes, performances and more! this year's theme is African Americans and the Arts.

Holocaust Survivor Speaker Event

On February the 16th Students are invited to attend a special speaker event in the library after 10th period on Friday, February 16 to hear the inspiring story of Holocaust survivor Sami Steigmann. All students are welcome to attend, whether you have questions or simply wish to listen and learn. Read more about him!

2024 Summer Youth Employment Program (SYEP) Lottery

apply by March 1st All students ages 14–24 are invited to apply to participate in the 2024 Summer Youth Employment Program (SYEP), which is now accepting applications for summer 2024 via the general lottery through March 1. Additional Guidance for Students with IEPs for the SYEP Application: Interested students should follow this TCAC Online Application Guide and submit an online application for their age group through their borough’s Transition and College Access Center (TCAC) office by 5:00 p.m. on March 1. TCACs will support students with IEPs and 504 Plans with the necessary information to be added to their applications in order to receive targeted support for placement in the TCAC-specific lottery; this enhances their chances of being placed in their respective borough and program choice. In addition, note the following: TCACs will support students with IEPs and 504 Plans with the necessary information to be added to their applications in order to receive targeted support for placement in the TCAC-specific lottery; this enhances their chances of being placed in their respective borough and program choice. In addition, note the following: Students ages 16–21 should submit a completed SYEP Older Youth Participant Application; and Students ages 14–15 should submit a completed paper SYEP Younger Youth Participant Application. For borough-specific questions regarding applications for students with IEPs or 504 Plans, contact your TCAC office; for general questions, email nyctcacs@schools.nyc.gov.

Apply for the Women's Bond Club Scholarship worth $40K

Apply by the 25th of March The Women's Bond Club Scholarship was established to assist young women pursuing careers in business, technology and finance in a Wall Street firm, bank, or service provider. The award is a $10,000 renewable scholarship, along with a mandatory mentoring program and internship possibilities. It is a really fantastic opportunity for young women pursuing this career trajectory; I had the pleasure of working with several winners during my time at another NYC high school. Stuy students first became eligible to compete for this award three years ago, and we have had winners each of those years! This is a scholarship eligible students should not ignore.
